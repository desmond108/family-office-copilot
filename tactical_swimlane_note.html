Tactical instructions — who does what, and when

The end-to-end process across three actors. The You lane is what the analyst does; the Copilot lane happens automatically. Each confirmed instruction now carries an enforcement tier — 🔒 enforced / 📡 monitored / 📝 advisory.

?

Does “Confirm items” fill my Allocations & Limits?

By itself, no. Confirm items commits the interpreted instructions — it produces a monitoring watchlist (triggers) and guidance folded into the proposal. It writes into no field.

Allocations are filled only by a second, separate click: if the instructions named target weights (“Gold ETF: 20%”), a 📥 Proposed allocation panel appears with an Apply to allocation targets button (Step 6). You still verify and adjust. Limits (band tolerance, liquidity, FX, position caps) are always manual — never extracted.

The order matters: set the mandate, risk and allocations first (Steps 1–6), then press Analyse (Step 7). They're technically independent, but analysing before the policy is set computes against the default preset — which looks wrong until you fix it; doing the policy first means the first analysis already reflects the client's policy. Only the review & proposal views (Steps 8–9) need the parsed book, and later tweaks recompute live.

One exception (v8): a 🔒 enforced price trigger — a stated absolute level on a priceable instrument, e.g. “buy gold below $4,000” — does reach the numbers. When you Analyse, the copilot checks the live price and gates the matching rebalance row (a buy above the level becomes a HOLD), using a figure with provenance. Confirm still writes no field itself; the enforced check runs at Analyse, so confirm the trigger before Step 7. Everything else stays 📡 monitored or 📝 advisory and never touches a figure.
