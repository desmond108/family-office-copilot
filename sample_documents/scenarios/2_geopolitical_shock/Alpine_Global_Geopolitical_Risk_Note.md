## Summary

The escalation in the Middle East has moved markets into a **risk-off** regime. Our
base case is elevated volatility for at least the next several weeks, with a right-tail
risk to energy prices if supply routes are disrupted.

## Cross-asset read

- **Safe havens bid.** Gold, the US dollar and US Treasuries are drawing flows. Gold in
  particular tends to lead during flight-to-safety episodes and offers a low-correlation
  hedge to the equity sleeve.
- **Equity volatility elevated.** Broad indices are choppy; drawdowns can overshoot
  fundamentals. We would **not** be adding to risk assets into the turmoil, even on
  mechanical pullback triggers, until realised volatility subsides.
- **Cash is optionality.** Holding dry powder in a money-market fund preserves the
  ability to act once dislocation creates value, rather than catching a falling knife.
- **Alternatives / uncorrelated exposure.** Where a mandate permits, uncorrelated and
  defensive alternatives can cushion the book; for cost- and liquidity-sensitive clients,
  liquid gold exposure is the pragmatic hedge.

## Recommended tilt

Lean **defensive**: raise gold and cash at the margin, defer discretionary equity adds,
and review any single-region or single-name concentration exposed to the conflict.
