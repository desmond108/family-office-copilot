Tactical instructions — who does what, and when

The end-to-end process across three actors. The You lane is what the analyst does; the Copilot lane happens automatically. In v10 the client's tactical text passes verbatim into one self-contained prompt — no sorting, no classifying — that the AI Model writes the proposal from.

?

How do the client's tactical instructions reach the proposal?

Verbatim, in one prompt. The tactical text goes straight to the AI Model — together with the intake parameters, the deterministic FACTS, the parsed holdings + statement source, and the research / other documents in full. There is no classification, review table, or enforcement step in between.

The prompt is the transparency surface. It's shown on the Proposal page — editable, copyable, downloadable — so you (and the client) can read exactly what the system asks the AI Model to do, and paste it into any AI model to reproduce the proposal and compare. Generate in-app runs the live AI model.

Numbers stay deterministic. Every figure is computed by the engine (the FACTS block); the AI writes prose only. Qualitative claims drawn from the documents are context — not independently verified.

The order matters: set the mandate, risk and allocations first (Steps 1–2), then press Analyse (Step 4), so the first analysis already reflects the client's policy. Limits (band tolerance, liquidity, FX, position caps) are always manual.
