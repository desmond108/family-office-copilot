# Meridian Wealth Planning — US Tax-Policy Brief for Non-US Persons

*House planning note · Q3 2026 · SYNTHETIC EXAMPLE — FOR TESTING (no real client data; general information, not personalised tax advice)*

## What changed

The US administration has moved to **raise taxes affecting foreign investors** in US
assets. Two effects matter most for a non-US-person private client holding US equities:

- **Dividend withholding.** Withholding on US-source dividends rises; absent full treaty
  relief, a non-US holder can face up to **30%** on distributions from US-domiciled funds
  and single stocks.
- **US estate exposure.** **US-situs assets** (US-domiciled ETFs and US single stocks)
  above a low threshold can be exposed to **US estate tax** for a non-US person — a
  material, often-overlooked drag.

## Mitigants (implementation, not a change of strategy)

- **Non-US-domiciled wrappers.** Irish-domiciled **UCITS** ETFs tracking the S&P 500 /
  Nasdaq-100 typically suffer **15%** underlying withholding (treaty) and are **non-US-
  situs** for estate purposes — materially reducing both drags while keeping the same
  market exposure.
- **Accumulating share classes** roll income internally, reducing distribution friction.
- **Trim US-situs concentration at the margin** rather than exiting the exposure; lean
  modestly toward **non-US-situs** holdings (e.g., gold) where consistent with the plan.
- **Avoid gain-triggering churn.** Prefer routing *new* money and the phased build into
  efficient wrappers over selling appreciated US-situs positions purely for tax reasons.
