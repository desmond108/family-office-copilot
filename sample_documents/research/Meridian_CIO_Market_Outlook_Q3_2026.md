MERIDIAN FAMILY OFFICE — CHIEF INVESTMENT OFFICE
Quarterly Market Outlook · Q3 2026
Classification: Confidential · House view for client discussion

SUMMARY VIEW
------------
- We remain **moderately constructive** on multi-asset portfolios but expect
  elevated volatility through the second half of 2026. Our stance is to stay
  invested to policy while holding a larger-than-usual liquidity buffer to fund
  opportunistic additions on weakness.
- US large-cap equity valuations are **stretched** (S&P 500 forward P/E in the top
  decile of its 20-year range). We would not chase the index here; we prefer to
  **scale in on pullbacks** rather than deploy in a single tranche.
- We see **US rates near their cycle peak**. The market prices modest cuts into
  2027, but sticky services inflation keeps the risk two-sided. Medium-duration
  high-quality bonds offer an attractive carry-to-risk trade-off; we favour a
  laddered / tranche-based build rather than a single duration bet.
- **Gold** retains its role as a portfolio diversifier and real-rate hedge. We are
  comfortable with a strategic 5–10% allocation and would add on dips toward the
  USD 4,000/oz area.

ASSET-CLASS POSITIONING
-----------------------
Equities — NEUTRAL, quality bias.
  Prefer profitable, cash-generative franchises and equal-weight exposures over
  cap-weighted concentration. For clients targeting US beta (Nasdaq-100, S&P 500),
  we suggest defining a pullback threshold in advance (e.g. 15–20% off the recent
  high) and adding in steps, keeping undeployed cash in a USD money-market fund.

Fixed income — MODEST OVERWEIGHT duration, up-in-quality.
  A 4–6 year effective-duration sleeve in US Treasuries / high-grade credit is our
  base case. Given the two-sided rate risk, build the position in tranches over
  4–8 weeks rather than at once. Emphasise low total expense and daily liquidity.

Commodities / Gold — STRATEGIC HOLD, add on weakness.
  5–10% gold via a physically-backed, low-fee ETF. Prefer accumulation below
  USD 4,000/oz; avoid chasing strength.

Cash / liquidity — ABOVE NEUTRAL.
  A USD money-market fund is the preferred parking vehicle for undeployed equity
  allocations and near-term liquidity needs.

FEES & IMPLEMENTATION
---------------------
Across all sleeves we prioritise **low total expense ratios and deep secondary
liquidity**. For core beta and duration, broad, large-AUM ETFs typically deliver
the best cost/liquidity profile; avoid complex or capacity-constrained wrappers
unless there is a clear, documented reason.

RISKS TO THE VIEW
-----------------
Re-acceleration of inflation (hawkish repricing), a growth scare that pressures
credit, and event-driven equity drawdowns. Each argues for the same discipline:
pre-committed thresholds, tranche-based execution, and a healthy cash buffer.
