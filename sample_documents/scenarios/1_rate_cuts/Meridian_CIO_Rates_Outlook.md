# Meridian CIO — Rates Outlook: Positioning for a Fed Pivot

*House research · Chief Investment Office · Q3 2026 · SYNTHETIC EXAMPLE — FOR TESTING (no real client data, not investment advice)*

## View in brief

We move to a **duration-friendly** stance. Disinflation has continued for three
consecutive quarters and the labour market is cooling without breaking. We now expect
the US Federal Reserve to begin an easing cycle, delivering on the order of **~100 bp of
cuts over the next 12 months**. The front end is likely to fall faster than the long end
— a **bull-steepening** of the curve.

## Implications for the sleeves

- **Fixed income — extend and build.** Reinvestment risk now outweighs price risk. We
  favour rotating the bond sleeve from short toward **medium duration** to lock in
  today's yields before they roll lower. A **tranched** build (e.g., three to four
  entries over the coming months) smooths entry and suits clients accumulating ahead of
  a known inflow.
- **Money market — a diminishing perch.** Cash yields will decline as the policy rate
  falls; parking liquidity here becomes progressively more expensive in opportunity
  terms. Use it as a staging area for the bond build, not a destination.
- **Gold.** A lower real-rate path is a tailwind. No change to a disciplined accumulation
  approach on defined levels.
- **US equity.** Lower discount rates are supportive, but valuation discipline still
  applies; a rules-based pullback entry remains sensible.

## What this note is (and is not)

Context for the write-up and the client conversation — a house macro view. It is **not**
a source of portfolio figures: every number in any proposal must come from the client's
parsed statements and the deterministic engine.
