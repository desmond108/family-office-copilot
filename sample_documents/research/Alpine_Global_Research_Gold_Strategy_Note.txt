Strategy Note — Gold: Positioning and Levels into H2 2026
Date: 8 July 2026 | Analyst desk | Synthetic example for testing

BOTTOM LINE
-----------
We maintain a constructive medium-term view on gold as a portfolio diversifier and
real-rate / geopolitical hedge. We would treat the USD 4,000/oz area as a sensible
accumulation zone for strategic buyers and would avoid chasing sharp rallies.

DRIVERS
-------
1. Real rates: with the US policy rate near its cycle peak and cuts expected into
   2027, the opportunity cost of holding a non-yielding asset should ease.
2. Central-bank demand: official-sector buying has been a persistent structural
   bid and shows little sign of reversing.
3. Diversification: gold's correlation to both equities and bonds has been low to
   negative during recent risk-off episodes, improving portfolio efficiency.

LEVELS & IMPLEMENTATION
-----------------------
- Strategic allocation: 5–10% of a diversified portfolio is a reasonable range for
  a hedge without dominating risk.
- Accumulation: scaling in below USD 4,000/oz improves the entry profile; a single
  large purchase at elevated prices is less attractive given near-term volatility.
- Vehicle: a physically-backed, low-fee, highly liquid gold ETF is preferable to
  leveraged or synthetic structures for a buy-and-hold hedge. Prioritise a low
  total expense ratio and tight trading spreads.

RISKS
-----
A hawkish inflation surprise that lifts real yields, a sharp USD rally, or a
liquidity event that forces broad de-risking could all pressure gold in the short
term. These do not change the strategic case but argue for disciplined, staggered
entry rather than a single tranche.

WHAT WE ARE WATCHING
--------------------
- The path of US real yields and the shape of the front end of the curve.
- Momentum extremes: we would fade very stretched rallies and prefer to add on
  pullbacks toward the accumulation zone noted above.
